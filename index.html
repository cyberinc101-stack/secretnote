What is SecretNote?

SecretNote lets you send private encrypted messages that permanently delete themselves after being read once — or after a time limit you set. Unlike email or messaging apps, there is no archive, no backup, and no trace once a note is destroyed.

How the encryption works

Your note is encrypted in your browser using AES-256-GCM before anything is stored. The decryption key is placed in the URL fragment (after the # symbol) — browsers never send URL fragments to servers, so your key is invisible to anyone monitoring traffic. Decryption happens locally in your recipient's browser.

Time-based expiry

Set any expiry from 1 minute to 7 days, or enter a custom duration. An expired note is automatically deleted and cannot be read, even if the link was never opened. This is ideal for time-sensitive credentials and one-time codes.

Why not just send an email or text?

Emails and messages are stored permanently on servers, in backups, and on recipient devices. If any account is compromised later, sensitive messages can be exposed. SecretNote ensures the information ceases to exist the moment it has been received.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SecretNote truly private?

Yes. Your note is encrypted in your browser using AES-256-GCM. The decryption key exists only in the URL you share. Our servers see only encrypted ciphertext with no way to decrypt it.

What happens when the link expires?

The note is automatically deleted. Anyone opening the link after expiry sees a message that the note is gone. They cannot tell whether it expired or was already read.

Can I password-protect a note?

Yes. Use the Options section to add a password. The recipient must enter the correct password to read the note. Share the password via a separate channel for maximum security.

Can someone screenshot the note?

We cannot prevent screenshots. SecretNote protects against digital copies and forwarding — the link becomes permanently invalid after the first read. For highly sensitive content, use the password feature.

Where is the note stored?

The encrypted note is stored in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is stored on any external server. Clearing your browser site data for this domain will delete any notes you have created.
