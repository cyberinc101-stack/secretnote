Burn After Reading — Send a Message That Destroys Itself

Write it, share the link, and the moment your recipient reads it, it is gone. No copy. No trace. No archive.

A burn-after-reading message is a note that permanently destroys itself the moment the recipient opens it. Unlike emails, texts, or chat messages that linger in inboxes, backups, and server logs indefinitely, a burn-after-reading note ceases to exist the instant it has been read.

SecretNote gives you exactly this — encrypted, one-time links that self-destruct on first view — in your browser, with no account required.

How it works

You write a note → encrypted in your browser → one-time link generated → link shared with recipient → recipient opens it once → note permanently deleted
  1. Write your message in SecretNote. Your text is encrypted using AES-256-GCM directly in your browser — it never leaves your device unencrypted.
  2. Click Create Note. A unique one-time link is generated. The decryption key lives only in the URL after the # symbol, which browsers never transmit to servers.
  3. Copy and send the link to your recipient via any channel — email, WhatsApp, Slack, text, or anything else.
  4. The recipient clicks the link. A confirmation screen tells them the note will be destroyed on reading. They confirm, read it once, and it is immediately and permanently deleted from storage.
  5. Any attempt to open the link again returns a "note not found" message. The content is gone.

Time-based destruction: burn even if unread

You can also set a time limit on any note. If the recipient does not open it within your chosen window, it auto-destructs anyway. Options include:

  • 1, 5, 10, 20, or 30 minutes
  • 1 hour or 24 hours
  • 7 days
  • Any custom duration you enter

This is useful when you want to make sure a sensitive note cannot be accessed after a deadline, regardless of whether it was read.

Instant burn: destroy without a confirmation screen

By default, the recipient sees a confirmation step before the note is displayed — giving them a moment to realise they are about to read something that will disappear. If you prefer the note to self-destruct the instant the link is opened (no confirmation), enable the Skip confirmation option when creating the note.

Can screenshots prevent it from truly burning?

Yes — SecretNote cannot prevent a recipient from taking a screenshot. What it does prevent is digital forwarding, second-access, and indefinite storage. Once a note is burned, no one can retrieve it from our server, your browser cache, or any cloud backup. The value is in eliminating the passive risk of a note being found later, not in preventing a deliberate person from recording it.

For content where screenshots are a concern, use the optional passphrase feature and share the password verbally — so even if someone else clicks the link, they cannot read the note without the phrase you deliver separately.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a character limit?

Up to 50,000 characters per note — enough for long messages, credential blocks, or detailed instructions.

What does the recipient see if the note has already been read?

They see a message stating that the note has already been opened or has expired. They cannot tell which — just that it is gone.

Can I get a notification when my note is read?

An email notification on destroy feature is in development and will notify you when a note has been opened. For now, if the link stops working, the note has been read.

Do I need an account to use this?

No. Write your message, click Create Note, copy the link. No signup, no email address, no personal data collected.
